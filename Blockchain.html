What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a decentralized, distributed ledger technology that records transactions in a way that makes them difficult or impossible to alter, hack, or cheat.

Key Features of Blockchain

  • Decentralization: No single entity controls the blockchain network.
  • Security: Transactions are secure and tamper-proof.
  • Transparency: All transactions are publicly visible on the blockchain.

How Does Blockchain Work?

Blockchain uses cryptographic techniques to create a chain of blocks, each containing a record of transactions. When a new transaction is verified, it is added to the chain, creating a permanent record.

Applications of Blockchain

  • Cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other cryptocurrencies use blockchain technology.
  • Supply Chain Management: Blockchain can track the movement of products through the supply chain.
  • Voting Systems: Blockchain can provide secure and transparent voting systems.
  • Healthcare: Blockchain can be used to securely store and share medical records.
